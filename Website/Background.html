  Drug Action  
Specificity





Remdesivir (Purple)



Synthesizing drugs for a particular purpose is not an easy task - drugs are specific
to the problem they solve, as well as the involved receptors and membranes. As
an example, to the left you see the drug Remdesivir, able to assist in the treatment
of SARS-CoV-2 by blocking off viral replication - this drug is extremely specific
in its action, it works well against coronaviruses only because of the particular
mechanism they use to replicate and the specific nature of their genome. It would be futile
even in the face of another virus that replicates slightly differently, let alone a bacterial
or some other type of infection.

What this is meant to point out, is that the concept of drugs that are able to transcend
the boundaries of different diseases and somehow act as treatment tools to multiple
ailments is amazing, given how specific drugs seem to be to a particular metabolic/
molecular issue.






  Our Question  
Clinical Drug Multi-Functionality

As noted in the previous section, drugs are incredibly specific, so the notion of drugs that treat multiple diseases sounds
fascinating. But, we can only say that they "sound fascinating" as students who don't really know much about drugs, or
their mechanisms, or their production. Are such multi-functional drugs that treat more than one disease interesting to the
scientific community too? Or is there no real clinical specialty to such a product?

We seek to answer this by obtaining data about how many diseases each drug belonging to a general class of diseases
can treat. We then obtain information about how many clinical trials each of these drugs has been flagged in, and
mark the most frequently mentioned drugs with respect to clinical trials. From these two sets of info, represented
graphically, one can identify whether or not a drug that has a broader scope of use in terms of diseases is also
studied more extensively (that is, shown more interest) by the field of clinical drug testing.

You can find out more about how we acquire such information on our databases page, and you can test out this tool for a
variety of disease clusters on our main homepage.
